# MPLP MPGC Candidate Risk Matrix v0.1

## A. Purpose

Identify risks before MPGC candidate review.

## B. Risk Matrix

| Risk | Applies to | Why it matters | Mitigation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| premature normative promotion | all intake candidates | review materials could be misread as accepted protocol direction | repeat pre-RFC, non-normative, and not-accepted boundaries across all materials |
| schema creep | evidence vocabulary candidate; stop/escalation/revision candidate | vocabulary or posture questions could drift into implicit field requests | keep all schema-impact language negative and explicitly deferred |
| profile proliferation | stop / escalation / revision posture candidate | posture discussion could multiply profile-like artifacts too early | keep the candidate in holding form and require more evidence first |
| binding vocabulary overfit | evidence vocabulary candidate | vocabulary questions could overfit one implementation substrate | frame all mappings as questions, not changes |
| downstream evidence overgeneralization | all intake candidates | one anonymous downstream success can be misread as general proof | keep evidence strengths conservative and gap-focused |
| hidden runtime endorsement | lifecycle governance candidate; evidence vocabulary candidate | candidate wording could accidentally bless one runtime style | keep vendor-neutral/runtime-neutral wording only |
| product endorsement leakage | lifecycle governance candidate; stop/escalation/revision candidate | explanation language could drift toward product semantics | keep candidate scope abstract and boundary-led |
| certification implication | all intake candidates | review materials could be mistaken for certification-like authority | repeat no-certification-claim boundaries and avoid evaluative approval wording |
| conformance confusion | all intake candidates | review packet might be misread as new obligation | keep non-promotion audit and gate attached to the packet |
| implementation dependency | evidence vocabulary candidate; delegation/delivery/constraint family context | candidate language may stay too close to one implementation substrate | require explicit evidence of broader generality before any later work item |
| ambiguity between revision / approval / execution | lifecycle governance candidate; stop/escalation/revision candidate | semantic overlap would distort future evaluation | preserve explicit distinction language in review materials |
