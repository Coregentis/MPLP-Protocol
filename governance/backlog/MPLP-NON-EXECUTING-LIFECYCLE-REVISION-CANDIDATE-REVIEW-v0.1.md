# MPLP Non-Executing Lifecycle Revision Candidate Review v0.1

## A. Purpose

Review the non-executing lifecycle revision candidate as anonymous downstream
evidence-derived backlog item.

## B. Candidate Scope

Non-executing lifecycle revision after evidence insufficiency, stale context,
operator clarification, or contract-blocked states.

## C. Candidate Fit

| Fit dimension | Assessment | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| lifecycle relevance | `medium_high` | the candidate clearly concerns bounded lifecycle revision posture rather than raw task or content payload alone |
| evidence relevance | `high` | the candidate is strongly driven by evidence insufficiency, stale context, and clarification posture |
| Confirm / Trace / Plan / Context relationship | `partial_fit` | closest fit is Confirm-adjacent review posture and Trace-adjacent evidence vocabulary; Plan and Context remain surrounding surfaces rather than a direct envelope home |
| guide candidate potential | `medium` | explanatory disclosure and review-posture guidance could later emerge as a guide candidate |
| profile candidate potential | `medium` | if repeated across more downstream evidence, the lifecycle-revision posture family could become a future profile consideration |
| binding consideration potential | `medium_high` | the strongest short-term fit is vocabulary and relation binding around evidence insufficiency, clarification, and revision posture |
| schema impact | `no_change` | current evidence does not justify schema discussion |
| protocol law impact | `no_change` | the candidate remains non-normative and below protocol-law promotion |

## D. Recommended Disposition

Retain this candidate in backlog form and mark it as a future guide/profile/
binding consideration candidate, with the strongest near-term fit currently in
binding consideration around evidence vocabulary and relation wording.

## E. Explicit Non-Decisions

- no schema change
- no protocol law change
- no normative status
- no runtime endorsement
- no product endorsement
- no implementation dependency
- no certification claim
